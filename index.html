About Me

Hi! I'm a Ph.D. student in Anthropological Genetics at the University of Utah, exploring how genes hold the clues to human history and adaptations. My journey into this field has been anything but conventional—I started with a Bachelor's in Mechanical Engineering at BUET, transitioned into Bioinformatics for my Master's at UNC Charlotte, and now, I’m diving deep into the world of population genetics. I love uncovering the stories hidden in DNA and using computational tools to make sense of our evolutionary past!

Research

My My research focuses on developing innovative genomic methods to uncover both ancient and modern human adaptations. I specialize in creating new statistical approaches that merge concepts from population and statistical genetics, applying them to large-scale genomic datasets (e.g., UK Biobank) through computational analysis.
